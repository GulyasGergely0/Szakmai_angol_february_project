Fisherman's Bastion in Budapest, Hungary

The Fisherman's Bastion is a magnificent landmark in Budapest, Hungary, located on the Castle Hill, overlooking the Danube River. It was built in the late 19th century as part of the restoration of the Buda Castle complex, and it quickly became one of the most popular tourist attractions in the city.

The Fisherman's Bastion consists of seven towers, each of which represents one of the seven Hungarian tribes that founded the country in the 9th century. The towers are connected by a walkway that offers stunning views of the city, including the Parliament building and the Chain Bridge.

"The Fisherman's Bastion is a true gem of Budapest, and its panoramic views are simply breathtaking." - Lonely Planet

Visitors can explore the bastion for free, but there is a fee to enter the upper towers and terraces. The bastion is open year-round, and it is particularly beautiful at sunset when the lights of the city come on.
